Shuckle: The Mold Pokemon

Introduction

I promise I will try to be objective after this section. That said:

Easily, one of my favorite Pokemon. In the top ten without a second doubt. The overall design is simple, yet unique. It is said that Shuckle might be some sort of sentient mold inhabiting a meteorite, that in and of itself is pretty interesting. It's defense focused stats provides for a different style of combat than the standard hit-as-hard-as-you-can strategy proving that changing it up every now and then can prove fun and challanging. Below I will be attempting to explain which mechanical aspects of Shuckle convinced me to place it in a spot on my team.

Type

Bug and Rock type

Bug-type is known as a bit of an outlier in terms of poplarity, due to not having stellar stats in general -- known for being quite squishy and ineffective(as bugs are).
On average, Bug-types have the lowest base HP and base stat total of all types. Bug-type is tied with Grass as the most resisted type, with seven types resisting each of them. These resistant types are most effectively covered by Water-type attacks. On average, Bug Pokémon have the lowest base Special Attack of all types.


Rock-type is known for generally having decient physical stats. However, it is tied with grass in possessing the highest number of weaknesses. Rock-type Pokémon, on average, have the lowest Speed of all Pokémon and of fully evolved Pokémon. The biggest drawback of Rock-type moves is the large percentage of moves which have less than 100% accuracy.

The combination of Bug-type and Rock-type nullifies most of the weaknesses and resistances, making them neutral, of both. Undeniably for the better. Shuckle proves that unlikely type-matchup, even contradictory ones, can prove advantageous.

    Stats

    Shuckle's stats are what make him the powerhouse he is, atleast in terms of defense and stalling a match, boasting a meaty def and sp. def allowing him to take even super-effective hits as a mere glancing blow. This is further compounded by EV training his HP and Atk stats making him capable of mounting a moderate counter-attack while stalling during a poison-stall or delivering stat-debuffs before switching to a more atk focused pokemon. His spd stat is so low that you're almost always guaranteed the 2nd attack, which might seem detrimental, but combined with certain moves that boost damage if hit first in the same turn it can turn advantageous.
